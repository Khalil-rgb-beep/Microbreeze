Course Overview

Microbiology is the study of microorganisms, which are tiny living organisms that can not be be seen with the naked eye. This website will cover the basics of microbiology, which are but not limited to the structure found in microbes, their taxonomy, and the difference between gram-positive and gram-negative bacterias.

What is a Eubacteria?

Eubacteria, also known as true bacterias represent prokaryotes with a rigid cell wall, flagella for motility, and other metabolic process needed, as stated in the article from CD Genomics. Furthermore, true bacterias have cell walls composed of peptidoglycan while archaeabacteria do not

Gram-positive vs. Gram-negative

The difference between gram-positive and gram-negative bacterias lies in their structure and composition of their cell wall. Hence, by knowing these characteristics one can easily distinguish them from one another

Identification of the bacteria

In this very moment, several biochemical tests exist to differentiate different species of from another. However, in my opinion, the most important would be the Gram stain, the laboratory used to distinguish gram-positive and gram-negative bacterias by taking note of the characteristics of their cell wall

Branches of Microbiology

There are different branches under microbiology, each branch of microbiology focuses on one particular microorganism such as bacteria, fungi, virus, protozoans, etc.

Bacteriology

As stated by Britannica. it is a branch under microbiology that focuses on the study of bacteria. Furthermore, there are a plethora of specializations under bacteriology such as agricultural, clinical, industrial, and systematic bacteriology

Mycology

Mycology is a branch under microbiology that deals with the study of fungi. They are eukaryotic organisms that mainly reproduce either through sexual or asexually. Moreover, they have a great impact in our day-to-day lives, in accordance with Zaragoza, 2017.

Virology

According to Mestrovic (n.d.), virology is a branch under microbiology that dwells in the study of viruses and virus-like agents' taxonomy, disease-producing properties, cultivation, and genetics
